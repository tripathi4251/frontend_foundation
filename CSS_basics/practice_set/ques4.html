Importance of enviornment

Life Support Systems

The environment provides the "ecosystem services" that we often take for granted but cannot survive without: Air Purification: Plants and trees perform photosynthesis, absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing the oxygen we breathe. Water Cycle: Natural landscapes like forests and wetlands filter pollutants from water and manage the cycle of evaporation and precipitation. Food Security: Soil health and pollinators (like bees and bats) are responsible for the vast majority of the global food supply.

Human Health

A degraded environment leads directly to a decline in human well-being: Pollution Control: Clean air and water reduce the incidence of respiratory diseases, waterborne illnesses, and chemical toxicity. Medicine: A huge percentage of modern medicines are derived from plant and microbial life found in diverse ecosystems like rainforests. Mental Well-being: Access to green spaces is scientifically proven to reduce stress, anxiety, and depression.

Climate Regulation

The environment acts as a massive "buffer" for the planet’s temperature. Oceans and forests function as carbon sinks, absorbing excess heat and greenhouse gases. Without these natural systems, global warming would accelerate at a pace that would make most of the planet uninhabitable.
