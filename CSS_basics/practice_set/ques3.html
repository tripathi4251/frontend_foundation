Bugatti

The Tourbillon is the successor to the Chiron and represents a massive shift in engineering. While the Veyron and Chiron were famous for their quad-turbocharged W16 engines, the Tourbillon introduces an entirely new powertrain. The Engine: A naturally aspirated 8.3-liter V16 (developed with Cosworth) that revs to 9,000 RPM. The Hybrid System: It is paired with three electric motors (two on the front axle, one on the rear) for a combined output of 1,800 hp. Performance: 0–100 km/h: 2.0 seconds 0–400 km/h: Under 25 seconds

Ferrari

Lamborghini is currently in a major transition period, having moved its entire lineup to hybrid power as of 2026. The brand's focus is on "High Performance Electrified Vehicles" (HPEV), combining their traditional high-revving engines with advanced electric motors.
