Computer science is the study of information, computation, and automation. While many people associate it strictly with building computers or writing code, it is actually a broad discipline focused on how we represent data and the algorithms we use to process it. Think of it as the bridge between theoretical logic and the physical machines that power our world.

At its heart, CS is a branch of mathematics. It asks: Is this problem solvable? And if so, how much time and memory will it take to solve? This is known as Computational Complexity.
